Pigeon Care: Feeding, Housing, Breeding & Common Diseases

For beginners, hobbyists & breeders • Reading time: 10–12 minutes
Birds Backyard care Simple English

Pigeons are intelligent, social birds kept for hobby, sport, or as pets. Proper care ensures healthy birds, regular breeding, and disease prevention.

This guide explains pigeon care including feeding, housing, breeding, health management, and disease prevention in simple, beginner-friendly language.

1. Understanding pigeon behaviour

Pigeons are social birds and prefer living in pairs or small flocks. Observing behaviour helps detect early signs of illness or stress.

Signs of healthy pigeons

  • Active, alert and moving freely
  • Clean feathers and bright eyes
  • Regular eating and drinking
  • Pair bonding and cooing sounds

Signs of stress or illness

  • Lethargy or sitting alone
  • Ruffled or dirty feathers
  • Loss of appetite
  • Abnormal droppings
  • Laboured breathing or sneezing

2. Housing and loft setup

Proper housing protects pigeons from predators, bad weather, and disease. Pigeons need clean, dry, and well-ventilated lofts.

Key housing points

  • Provide enough space for flying and resting (1–2 sq. ft per pigeon inside)
  • Elevated perches for resting and roosting
  • Separate breeding boxes (1 per pair)
  • Clean bedding regularly (sawdust or straw)
  • Secure fencing to prevent predators

Temperature and ventilation

  • Keep loft warm in winter and shaded in summer
  • Ensure proper airflow to avoid dampness and respiratory problems

3. Feeding pigeons

Pigeons require a balanced diet of grains, seeds, and supplements for growth, breeding, and good health.

Essential feed

  • Grain mix: wheat, maize, millet, sorghum
  • Legumes: peas, lentils, chickpeas in moderation
  • Grit and small stones to aid digestion
  • Fresh clean water daily

Supplements

  • Mineral and vitamin mix for breeding and moulting
  • Calcium for strong eggshells

Feeding tips

  • Provide feed 2–3 times a day
  • Remove spoiled or wet feed
  • Keep feeders and water containers clean

4. Breeding and egg care

  • Provide nesting boxes for each pair
  • Collect eggs daily if needed, or allow hens to incubate
  • Provide clean nesting material (straw or soft wood shavings)
  • Separate weak or sick birds from breeding pairs

5. Common pigeon diseases and prevention

Early detection is crucial. Maintain hygiene and regular observation to prevent disease outbreaks.

Common diseases

  • Canker (Trichomoniasis)
  • Salmonella infection
  • Respiratory infections (Mycoplasma, Chlamydia)
  • Parasites: lice, mites, worms
  • Egg binding in hens

Signs to call a vet

  • Lethargy or sudden death
  • Persistent diarrhea or abnormal droppings
  • Laboured breathing or coughing
  • Loss of appetite
  • Swollen joints or difficulty flying

Never use human medications without veterinary supervision. Always consult a licensed veterinarian.

6. Daily routine and hygiene

  • Provide fresh water and feed daily
  • Remove wet bedding and clean loft regularly
  • Check birds for signs of illness or injury
  • Maintain clean nesting boxes
  • Ensure proper ventilation and dryness

7. Summary

Pigeon care involves feeding, housing, breeding, and health monitoring. With clean lofts, balanced nutrition, and early disease detection, pigeons stay healthy, productive, and happy.
