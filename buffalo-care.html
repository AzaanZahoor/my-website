Buffalo Care: Feeding, Housing, Behaviour & Common Diseases

For beginners and farmers • Reading time: 10–12 minutes
Large animals Farm care Simple English

Buffaloes are strong, hardy animals that provide milk, meat, and labor. Proper care ensures high productivity, long life, and good health.

This guide covers feeding, housing, behaviour, common diseases, and preventive care for buffaloes in simple, beginner-friendly language.

1. Understanding buffalo behaviour

Buffaloes are social and intelligent. Observing their behaviour can help detect stress, illness, or discomfort early.

Signs of healthy buffaloes

  • Bright, alert eyes and smooth coat
  • Regular eating, drinking, and rumination
  • Calm, steady movement and posture
  • Normal social interaction with herd

Signs of stress or illness

  • Loss of appetite or sudden weight loss
  • Lethargy or unusual isolation from herd
  • Swelling, wounds, or limping
  • Loose or abnormal dung
  • Restlessness or excessive vocalizations

2. Housing and environment

Buffaloes need clean, dry, and spacious shelters to prevent disease and improve comfort.

Key housing points

  • Provide shade and shelter from rain, heat, and cold
  • Ensure proper ventilation
  • Use clean bedding like straw or dry soil
  • Separate sick or injured animals
  • Keep floor dry to prevent hoof problems

3. Feeding buffaloes

A balanced diet rich in roughage, supplemented with minerals, ensures health, milk production, and energy.

Diet essentials

  • Good-quality green fodder: grass, legumes
  • Dry fodder: hay, wheat straw
  • Concentrates: grains or commercial feed for energy
  • Mineral and salt blocks for trace elements
  • Fresh water available at all times

Feeding tips

  • Feed multiple times daily instead of one large portion
  • Monitor appetite and eating behaviour
  • Clean feeding troughs to prevent contamination

4. Hooves and foot care

  • Inspect hooves regularly for cracks, swelling, or infections
  • Trim hooves periodically by a professional farrier
  • Maintain dry floors to prevent foot rot

5. Common buffalo diseases and prevention

Buffaloes are prone to digestive, respiratory, and parasitic diseases.

Common health problems

  • Digestive disorders like bloating or diarrhea
  • Foot rot and lameness
  • Respiratory infections
  • Parasites: worms, ticks, lice
  • Milk fever or mineral deficiency

When to call a vet

  • Loss of appetite or sudden lethargy
  • Persistent diarrhea or constipation
  • Swelling, limping, or wounds
  • High fever, nasal discharge, or coughing

Always consult a veterinarian before administering any medicines or supplements.

6. Daily buffalo care routine

  • Check appetite, water intake, and dung daily
  • Provide clean water and balanced fodder
  • Clean shelter and bedding
  • Inspect hooves, body, and skin for injuries
  • Maintain vaccination and deworming schedule

7. Summary

Buffalo care involves balanced feeding, clean housing, hoof and health management, and regular observation. Following these practices ensures healthy, productive, and long-lived buffaloes.
