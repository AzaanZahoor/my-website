Goose Care: Feeding, Housing, Behaviour & Common Diseases

For beginners and small-scale poultry farmers • Reading time: 10–12 minutes
Poultry Farm care Simple English

Geese are hardy birds that provide eggs, meat, and natural pest control. Proper care ensures good health, productivity, and long life.

This guide covers feeding, housing, behaviour, common diseases, and preventive care for geese in a simple, beginner-friendly way.

1. Understanding goose behaviour

Geese are social and territorial birds. Observing their behaviour helps detect stress, illness, or discomfort early.

Signs of healthy geese

  • Active, alert, and responsive to surroundings
  • Good appetite and regular drinking
  • Feathers clean and shiny
  • Normal walking, swimming, and grazing behaviour

Signs of stress or illness

  • Lethargy or isolation from flock
  • Loss of appetite or sudden weight loss
  • Drooping wings or ruffled feathers
  • Diarrhoea or abnormal droppings
  • Excessive noise or aggressive behaviour

2. Housing and environment

Geese need dry, clean, and secure shelters. Protecting them from predators and extreme weather is essential.

Key housing points

  • Provide a well-ventilated coop or shed
  • Keep bedding dry using straw or wood shavings
  • Allow access to a pond or water source for swimming
  • Separate sick or injured birds
  • Protect against foxes, dogs, and other predators

3. Feeding geese

Geese require a balanced diet of greens, grains, and occasional protein supplements for healthy growth and egg production.

Diet essentials

  • Fresh greens and pasture: grass, clover, weeds
  • Grains: corn, wheat, barley, or commercial poultry feed
  • Clean water always available
  • Grit or small stones for digestion
  • Calcium supplements for laying geese (eggshell strength)

Feeding tips

  • Feed small portions several times a day
  • Monitor water intake and droppings
  • Keep feeders and waterers clean to prevent disease

4. Health and common goose diseases

Regular observation and proper care prevent most diseases. Geese can suffer from digestive, respiratory, and parasitic infections.

Common issues

  • Worms and external parasites like lice
  • Respiratory infections: sneezing, nasal discharge
  • Diarrhoea due to poor diet or dirty water
  • Egg-laying issues due to calcium deficiency
  • Injuries from pecking or fights

When to call a vet

  • Loss of appetite or lethargy
  • Persistent diarrhea or respiratory symptoms
  • Injuries or sudden behaviour change
  • Unusual feather loss or drooping wings

Never use human medications without veterinary advice. Always consult a licensed veterinarian for treatment.

5. Daily goose care routine

  • Check food, water, and droppings daily
  • Provide fresh greens and grains
  • Clean coop and bedding regularly
  • Observe behaviour for early signs of stress or illness
  • Monitor egg production and quality

6. Summary

Good goose care involves balanced feeding, clean housing, predator protection, and regular observation. By following these practices and consulting a veterinarian when necessary, geese stay healthy, productive, and happy.
