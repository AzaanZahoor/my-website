Cat Care: Feeding, Grooming, Litter Training, Behaviour, Diseases & Prevention

For beginners, pet owners & families • Reading time: 10–12 minutes
Pet animals Home care Simple English

Cats are clean, intelligent and independent animals. They adapt well to homes and can be wonderful companions. But proper care is important—cats need balanced food, a clean litter box, occasional grooming, safe indoor space and regular health checkups.

This complete guide explains cat feeding, grooming, behaviour, diseases, litter training and daily care steps in simple English so beginners can easily understand.

1. Understanding cat behaviour

Cats communicate through body posture, tail movement, eyes and vocal sounds. Understanding behaviour helps avoid stress and improve bonding.

Signs of a happy and relaxed cat

  • Soft purring
  • Slow blinking (a sign of trust)
  • Relaxed tail or tail held upright
  • Playfulness and curiosity
  • Normal eating and grooming habits

Signs of stress or fear

  • Hissing, growling or hiding
  • Flattened ears
  • Puffed-up tail
  • Loss of appetite
  • Over-grooming or not grooming at all

2. Housing and comfortable environment

Cats love quiet, clean and safe areas. A good environment helps reduce stress and keeps them healthy.

Essential housing needs

  • Soft sleeping bed or blanket
  • Clean, private litter box area
  • Fresh water available all day
  • Scratching post to protect furniture
  • Safe, enclosed windows or balcony

Indoor vs outdoor cats

  • Indoor cats: Live longer and stay safer from diseases, cars and fights.
  • Outdoor cats: Need vaccinations, parasite control and safe shelter.

3. Feeding cats properly

Cats are carnivores—they need animal-based protein. A balanced diet supports strong muscles, shiny coat, healthy digestion and immunity.

What cats should eat

  • High-quality cat food (dry or wet)
  • Boiled chicken or fish (no bones)
  • Eggs in small amounts
  • Cat treats (moderation)
  • Fresh clean water always available

Foods dangerous for cats

  • Chocolate
  • Onions and garlic
  • Alcohol or caffeine
  • Chicken bones
  • Milk for adult cats (causes diarrhoea)

Feeding schedule

  • Kittens: 3–4 meals a day
  • Adults: 2 meals a day
  • Senior cats: small, frequent meals

4. Litter training cats

Cats naturally prefer using litter because they like to stay clean. With a little guidance, they learn quickly.

Litter box tips

  • Place it in a quiet corner
  • Keep it clean—scoop daily
  • Use unscented, soft litter
  • Have one extra box (2 cats = 3 boxes)
  • Avoid feeding area near litter

Why cats avoid litter box

  • Dirty litter box
  • Stress or fear
  • Urinary infection
  • Wrong type of litter

5. Grooming and hygiene

Cats groom themselves, but they still need help to stay healthy—especially long-haired breeds.

Basic grooming tasks

  • Brush coat 2–3 times a week
  • Trim nails every few weeks
  • Clean ears gently
  • Occasional bath (if needed)
  • Check for fleas and ticks

Common grooming issues

  • Hairballs
  • Knots in fur (long-haired cats)
  • Dirty ears
  • Flea infestation

6. Common diseases and health problems

Cats hide illness well, so observe behaviour daily.

Common cat health issues

  • Respiratory infections
  • Diarrhoea or vomiting
  • Urinary infections
  • Worms and parasites
  • Skin allergies
  • Dental problems

Warning signs requiring vet attention

  • No eating for more than 24 hours
  • Continuous vomiting
  • Blood in urine or difficulty peeing
  • Lethargy or hiding all day
  • Severe injury or swelling

Never give human medicines to cats—they can be dangerous.

7. Prevention and daily care routine

  • Keep environment clean and quiet
  • Feed balanced cat food
  • Fresh water available all day
  • Clean litter box daily
  • Keep vaccination updated
  • Regular grooming and health checks
  • Avoid stress and loud noises

8. Summary

Cat care is simple—clean space, balanced food, daily observation, and lots of love. By following this guide and consulting a veterinarian when needed, your cat will stay healthy, confident and happy.
