Dog Care: Feeding, Training, Grooming, Behaviour, Hygiene, Diseases & Prevention

For beginners, owners & pet lovers • Reading time: 10–14 minutes
Pet animals Home care Simple English

Dogs are loyal, intelligent and affectionate animals. They require proper feeding, daily exercise, grooming, mental stimulation and health checks to live a long and happy life. A well-cared dog becomes a loving companion and remains active, playful and friendly.

This guide explains all important parts of dog care—behaviour, nutrition, training, exercise, grooming, hygiene, diseases and prevention—in simple, beginner-friendly language.

1. Understanding dog behaviour

Dogs express feelings through tail movement, ears, eyes and body posture. Understanding their signals helps in proper training and care.

Healthy behaviour signs

  • Wagging tail and relaxed body
  • Active, friendly and curious
  • Good appetite and regular drinking
  • Normal barking, playing and social behaviour
  • Clean eyes and shiny coat

Warning signs

  • Loss of appetite
  • Continuous scratching or shaking head
  • Limping or difficulty walking
  • Heavy breathing or coughing
  • Sudden aggression or fearfulness
  • Isolation from family members

2. Housing and safe environment

Dogs need a clean, comfortable and secure place to rest. Proper housing prevents stress and health problems.

Basic housing requirements

  • Soft bedding or mat for sleeping
  • A dry, shaded and well-ventilated area
  • Separate feeding and resting space
  • No sharp objects around
  • Clean bowls for food and water

Indoor vs outdoor dogs

  • Indoor dogs: Need regular walks, playtime and training.
  • Outdoor dogs: Need strong shelter to protect from heat, cold and rain.

3. Feeding dogs the right way

A dog’s health depends heavily on what it eats. Balanced nutrition keeps their coat shiny, muscles strong and immunity high.

What dogs should eat

  • High-quality dog food or home-cooked balanced diet
  • Protein sources (eggs, chicken, fish)
  • Vegetables like carrots, pumpkin, peas
  • Rice, oats or chapati in small amounts
  • Clean drinking water always available

Things dogs must NOT eat

  • Chocolate
  • Onions and garlic
  • Grapes and raisins
  • Excess salt or spicy food
  • Alcohol or caffeine

Feeding tips

  • Puppies require 3–4 meals a day.
  • Adult dogs need 1–2 meals a day.
  • Avoid overfeeding to prevent obesity.
  • Introduce new foods slowly.

4. Exercise and mental stimulation

Dogs need daily exercise to stay strong and prevent behavioural problems.

Exercise ideas

  • Daily walks (20–60 minutes depending on breed)
  • Playing fetch
  • Running in open space
  • Interactive toys
  • Training sessions

Without exercise, dogs become bored, destructive and unhealthy.

5. Grooming and hygiene

Grooming keeps the dog clean and prevents skin infections, parasites and odour.

Daily or weekly grooming tasks

  • Brushing the coat
  • Checking ears for wax or redness
  • Trimming nails when needed
  • Bathing once every 2–4 weeks
  • Brushing teeth regularly

Common grooming problems

  • Hair shedding
  • Fleas and ticks
  • Ear infections
  • Dry or flaky skin

6. Basic dog training

Training makes dogs disciplined, confident and well-behaved. It also builds a strong bond between dog and owner.

Basic commands

  • Sit
  • Stay
  • Come
  • Heel (walk beside you)
  • No (stop unwanted actions)

Training tips

  • Use reward-based training
  • Never hit or shout at the dog
  • Keep sessions short and fun
  • Practice daily for consistency

7. Common diseases and health problems

Early detection of health problems prevents complications. Watch your dog closely every day.

Common health issues

  • Skin allergies
  • Ear infections
  • Worm infestation
  • Diarrhoea or vomiting
  • Coughing and respiratory infections
  • Lameness or joint pain

When to seek veterinary help

  • Loss of appetite for more than one day
  • Continuous diarrhoea or vomiting
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Severe injury or swelling
  • Unusual tiredness or collapse

Avoid giving human medicines without expert advice. Wrong medicines can harm dogs.

8. Prevention and daily care

  • Keep the dog’s environment clean
  • Feed fresh, good-quality food
  • Provide clean drinking water daily
  • Brush coat and check skin regularly
  • Keep vaccination records updated
  • Avoid contact with sick stray animals

9. Summary

Good dog care includes proper feeding, grooming, exercise, training and prompt veterinary attention. Dogs depend on their owners for love and responsible care. With daily attention and kindness, your dog will remain healthy, active and a loving member of your family.
