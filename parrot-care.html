Parrot Care: Feeding, Housing, Behaviour & Common Diseases

For beginners and pet owners • Reading time: 10–12 minutes
Birds Pet care Simple English

Parrots are intelligent, social, and long-lived birds. They require proper nutrition, housing, mental stimulation, and regular health checks to thrive as pets.

This guide explains parrot care including feeding, housing, behaviour management, common diseases, and daily routines in beginner-friendly language.

1. Understanding parrot behaviour

Parrots are highly social and communicate using vocalizations, body language, and mimicry. Observing behaviour helps identify stress, boredom, or illness early.

Signs of healthy parrots

  • Bright, alert eyes and smooth feathers
  • Active, playful, and responsive to surroundings
  • Regular eating and drinking
  • Normal preening and vocalizations

Signs of stress or illness

  • Feather plucking or sudden loss of feathers
  • Lethargy, hiding, or reduced interaction
  • Loss of appetite
  • Abnormal droppings or breathing problems
  • Unusual vocalizations or aggression

2. Housing and environment

Proper cage size and setup are crucial. Parrots need space to stretch wings, climb, and play.

Key housing points

  • Cage large enough for full wing extension and perching
  • Provide multiple perches at different heights
  • Place toys for mental stimulation and chewing
  • Keep cage away from drafts, direct sunlight, and toxic fumes
  • Clean cage regularly and provide fresh water daily

3. Feeding parrots

A balanced diet ensures good health, vibrant feathers, and longevity.

Essential diet

  • Pellets designed for parrots (main diet)
  • Fresh fruits: apple, pear, banana, berries (avoid seeds of some fruits)
  • Vegetables: carrot, spinach, broccoli, peppers
  • Occasional nuts as treats (in moderation)

Feeding tips

  • Provide fresh water daily
  • Remove uneaten food to prevent spoilage
  • Offer variety to prevent boredom

4. Mental stimulation and social interaction

  • Daily interaction with owner or other birds
  • Provide safe toys and foraging activities
  • Rotate toys regularly to maintain interest
  • Teach simple commands or tricks for enrichment

5. Common parrot diseases and prevention

Regular observation, hygiene, and diet prevent most health problems.

Common diseases

  • Psittacosis (Chlamydiosis)
  • Feather plucking due to stress or parasites
  • Beak and feather disease (PBFD)
  • Respiratory infections
  • Obesity from high-fat diet or low activity

Signs to consult a vet

  • Lethargy, lack of appetite, or sudden behavioural changes
  • Laboured breathing or discharge from eyes/nose
  • Abnormal droppings or sudden feather loss

Never give human medications without veterinary supervision. Consult an avian vet for accurate diagnosis and treatment.

6. Daily parrot care routine

  • Feed and provide fresh water daily
  • Interact for at least 1–2 hours
  • Clean cage, perches, and food/water bowls
  • Observe bird behaviour and droppings
  • Rotate toys and provide foraging opportunities

7. Summary

Proper parrot care involves balanced nutrition, safe housing, mental stimulation, and careful observation for early disease detection. Following these steps ensures a healthy, happy, and long-lived parrot.
