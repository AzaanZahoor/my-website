Donkey Care: Feeding, Housing, Behaviour & Common Diseases

For beginners and small-scale farmers • Reading time: 10–12 minutes
Large animals Farm care Simple English

Donkeys are hardy, intelligent animals that have been used as working animals for centuries. Proper care ensures good health, productivity, and longevity.

This guide covers donkey feeding, housing, behaviour, common diseases, and preventive care in simple, beginner-friendly language.

1. Understanding donkey behaviour

Donkeys are social, cautious, and intelligent. Observing behaviour helps detect stress, illness, or discomfort early.

Signs of a healthy donkey

  • Alert and responsive to surroundings
  • Regular eating and drinking
  • Calm, steady movement and posture
  • Shiny coat and bright eyes

Signs of stress or illness

  • Lethargy, refusal to eat, or excessive lying down
  • Unusual vocalizations, aggression, or anxiety
  • Swelling, wounds, or lameness
  • Loose or abnormal dung

2. Housing and environment

Donkeys need dry, safe, and shaded areas. Proper housing prevents foot and joint problems, skin infections, and stress.

Key housing points

  • Provide a shelter with a roof and dry floor
  • Keep bedding clean and dry
  • Allow enough space to move freely
  • Separate sick or injured animals
  • Protect from extreme heat, cold, and wet conditions

3. Feeding donkeys

Donkeys require a fibre-rich diet and clean water daily. Overfeeding concentrates can lead to obesity and digestive issues.

Diet essentials

  • Good-quality roughage: hay, straw, grass
  • Fresh water available at all times
  • Occasional mineral salt blocks for trace elements
  • Limit grains and high-calorie feeds

Feeding tips

  • Feed multiple small portions instead of one large meal
  • Observe eating habits daily
  • Clean feeding troughs to avoid contamination

4. Hooves and foot care

  • Check hooves daily for cracks, stones, or infections
  • Trim hooves every 6–8 weeks by a professional farrier
  • Keep floors dry to prevent hoof rot

5. Common donkey diseases and prevention

Donkeys can get digestive disorders, respiratory infections, skin diseases, and parasitic infections.

Common issues

  • Colic due to sudden feed changes
  • Parasites: worms, ticks, lice
  • Skin infections or sores from wet bedding
  • Lameness or foot rot
  • Respiratory problems in poorly ventilated shelters

When to call a vet

  • Loss of appetite or severe lethargy
  • Persistent diarrhoea or constipation
  • Lameness or swelling in limbs
  • High fever, nasal discharge, or coughing

Never give human medicines without veterinary supervision. Use only veterinary-prescribed treatments.

6. Daily donkey care routine

  • Observe behaviour, appetite, and dung
  • Provide fresh water and roughage
  • Clean shelter and bedding
  • Check hooves and body for wounds
  • Monitor weight and general health

7. Summary

Caring for donkeys requires attention to housing, diet, foot care, and behaviour observation. Regular preventive measures and veterinary care ensure a healthy, productive, and happy animal.
