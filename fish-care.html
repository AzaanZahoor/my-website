Fish Care: Feeding, Tank Setup, Breeding & Common Diseases

For beginners, hobbyists & aquarium enthusiasts • Reading time: 10–12 minutes
Aquatic animals Aquarium care Simple English

Fish are delicate and fascinating pets, but they need proper care to thrive. Healthy water conditions, balanced nutrition, and careful observation are essential for survival and longevity.

This guide explains fish care, including tank setup, feeding, breeding, common diseases, and daily maintenance in beginner-friendly language.

1. Understanding fish behaviour

Observing fish daily helps detect stress, illness, and compatibility issues. Healthy fish swim actively, explore the tank, and respond to feeding times.

Signs of healthy fish

  • Active swimming and regular feeding behaviour
  • Bright colours and clear eyes
  • Fins fully extended and moving normally
  • No abnormal growths, spots, or swelling

Signs of stress or illness

  • Clamped fins or hiding constantly
  • Loss of appetite or erratic swimming
  • White spots, fungus, or reddened areas on the body
  • Laboured breathing near the surface

2. Tank setup and environment

Proper tank setup is the foundation of fish health. Water quality, filtration, and tank size affect growth and lifespan.

Key points

  • Tank size according to fish type and number (avoid overcrowding)
  • Filtration system to remove waste and maintain water quality
  • Heating system for tropical fish to maintain stable temperature
  • pH and hardness levels suitable for species
  • Provide hiding spots and plants for comfort and stress reduction

Water maintenance

  • Change 20–30% of water weekly
  • Check ammonia, nitrite, nitrate, and pH levels regularly
  • Dechlorinate tap water before adding to tank

3. Feeding fish

Fish require species-specific diets to remain healthy. Overfeeding is a common cause of water pollution and disease.

Types of food

  • Flakes or pellets for daily feeding
  • Frozen or live food for protein (brine shrimp, bloodworms)
  • Vegetables for herbivorous species

Feeding tips

  • Feed small amounts 1–2 times daily
  • Remove uneaten food to prevent water contamination
  • Observe fish eating habits to detect illness early

4. Breeding and fry care

  • Provide a separate breeding tank to prevent egg or fry predation
  • Maintain optimal water temperature and quality for breeding
  • Feed breeders a high-protein diet
  • Use gentle filtration to protect delicate fry

5. Common fish diseases and prevention

Maintaining a clean tank and observing fish daily prevents most health issues.

Common diseases

  • Ich (white spot disease)
  • Fin rot
  • Fungal infections
  • Swim bladder disorders
  • Internal parasites

Signs to consult a vet or aquarium expert

  • Sudden lethargy or death
  • Discoloration or white patches
  • Loss of appetite
  • Abnormal swimming or floating upside down

Never use human medications in the tank without expert advice. Incorrect dosage can harm fish and disrupt tank balance.

6. Daily fish care routine

  • Check fish for signs of stress or illness
  • Ensure clean water and functioning filtration
  • Feed appropriately and remove uneaten food
  • Observe breeding behaviour and separate eggs if needed
  • Maintain stable temperature and lighting

7. Summary

Fish care involves proper feeding, clean water, suitable tank conditions, and careful observation. Following these principles ensures healthy, active fish and a thriving aquarium environment.
