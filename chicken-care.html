Chicken Care: Feeding, Housing, Behaviour, Egg Production, Common Diseases & Prevention

For beginners, backyard farmers & poultry enthusiasts • Reading time: 10–12 minutes
Poultry Farm care Simple English

Chickens are social, curious and productive animals. With proper care, they can provide eggs, meat and companionship. Poor care leads to disease, reduced egg production and high mortality.

This beginner-friendly guide explains chicken feeding, housing, behaviour, egg care, common diseases and prevention in simple English.

1. Understanding chicken behaviour

Chickens are flock animals—they thrive in groups and have a clear pecking order. Observing their behaviour helps detect illness early and maintain harmony.

Signs of healthy, happy chickens

  • Active and alert
  • Bright eyes and smooth feathers
  • Regular pecking and scratching
  • Roosting together calmly at night
  • Consistent egg-laying (if hens)

Signs of stress or illness

  • Lethargy or hiding
  • Pale comb and wattles
  • Loss of appetite
  • Ruffled feathers
  • Abnormal droppings

2. Housing and coop management

Chickens need a dry, safe and well-ventilated coop. Proper housing prevents predators, heat stress, frostbite, and diseases.

Key housing points

  • Provide enough space: 2–3 sq. ft per bird inside the coop and 8–10 sq. ft per bird in run
  • Good ventilation without drafts
  • Secure doors and fencing to prevent predators
  • Clean bedding (straw, wood shavings) changed regularly
  • Separate nesting boxes for hens (1 box per 3–4 hens)

Lighting and temperature

  • Chickens need 14–16 hours of light for optimal egg production
  • Protect from extreme heat; provide shade and water
  • In cold climates, insulate coop and use safe heat sources

3. Feeding chickens properly

A balanced diet keeps chickens healthy, ensures strong eggs and good growth. Feed quality grains, vegetables, and protein sources.

Essential feed

  • Layer feed for egg-laying hens
  • Starter/grower feed for chicks
  • Grains: corn, wheat, barley in moderation
  • Vegetables and green fodder
  • Fresh clean water at all times

Supplements

  • Calcium for strong eggshells (crushed oyster shells)
  • Grit to aid digestion
  • Vitamins during stress periods or disease recovery

Feeding tips

  • Feed small quantities multiple times a day
  • Remove wet or spoiled feed immediately
  • Keep feeders clean to avoid disease

4. Egg production and care

Proper care improves egg quality and reduces breakage.

Tips for egg care

  • Collect eggs 2–3 times a day
  • Store eggs in a clean, cool area
  • Clean nesting boxes regularly
  • Monitor hens for broodiness

5. Common diseases and prevention

Early detection of disease is crucial. Routine observation helps prevent losses.

Common poultry diseases

  • Respiratory infections (coughing, nasal discharge)
  • Parasites: lice, mites, worms
  • Egg binding
  • Fowl pox
  • Salmonella infection

Signs to call a vet immediately

  • Severe lethargy or collapse
  • Persistent diarrhea
  • Blood in droppings or abnormal eggs
  • Difficulty breathing or coughing

Avoid using human medicines on chickens. Always consult a veterinarian for treatment.

6. Daily care routine

  • Check water and feed daily
  • Clean coop and nesting boxes regularly
  • Observe chicken behaviour for early signs of illness
  • Rotate bedding and maintain dry floors
  • Provide dust bath area to prevent parasites

7. Summary

Chicken care requires attention to feeding, housing, hygiene and health. Proper routine and observation prevent disease, ensure egg production and improve chicken lifespan.
