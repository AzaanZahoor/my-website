Turkey Care: Feeding, Housing, Behaviour, Health & Egg Production

For backyard farmers, beginners & poultry enthusiasts • Reading time: 10–12 minutes
Poultry Farm care Simple English

Turkeys are large, social birds that require proper care for good growth, egg production, and overall health. Neglecting their needs can lead to poor growth, disease, and reduced productivity.

This guide explains turkey care including feeding, housing, behaviour, health management, and egg production in simple, beginner-friendly language.

1. Understanding turkey behaviour

Turkeys are social birds that establish a pecking order. Observing behaviour helps detect illness and maintain flock harmony.

Signs of healthy, happy turkeys

  • Active, alert and moving freely
  • Bright eyes and smooth feathers
  • Regular foraging and eating
  • Roosting together calmly at night
  • Consistent egg laying in hens

Signs of stress or disease

  • Lethargy or isolation
  • Pale or discolored wattles and snood
  • Loss of appetite or sudden weight loss
  • Ruffled feathers or constant preening
  • Abnormal droppings

2. Housing and space requirements

Proper housing ensures turkeys are safe from predators, harsh weather, and disease. They need enough space to move, roost, and forage.

Key housing points

  • Space: 4–5 sq. ft per turkey inside, 10 sq. ft per turkey outside
  • Well-ventilated and dry housing
  • Secure fencing to prevent predators
  • Clean bedding changed regularly
  • Separate nesting boxes (1 per 3–4 hens)

Temperature and lighting

  • Provide shade in hot weather and insulated shelter in cold
  • Natural daylight encourages egg production
  • Avoid damp areas to prevent respiratory diseases

3. Feeding turkeys for health and productivity

Turkeys require a balanced diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals for healthy growth and egg production.

Essential feed

  • Starter feed for poults (baby turkeys)
  • Grower feed for young turkeys
  • Layer feed for adult hens
  • Grains: maize, barley, wheat in moderation
  • Fresh vegetables and greens
  • Clean water available at all times

Supplements

  • Calcium for eggshell strength
  • Grit to aid digestion
  • Vitamin supplements during stress, moulting, or illness

Feeding tips

  • Feed small quantities multiple times a day
  • Remove wet or spoiled feed promptly
  • Keep feeders and waterers clean

4. Egg collection and care

  • Collect eggs daily to prevent breakage and contamination
  • Store eggs in a cool, dry place
  • Clean nesting boxes regularly
  • Monitor hens for broodiness and separate if needed

5. Common diseases and prevention

Regular observation is key. Early detection prevents losses and improves flock health.

Common turkey diseases

  • Respiratory infections (coughing, nasal discharge)
  • Parasites: mites, lice, worms
  • Blackhead disease (Histomoniasis)
  • Salmonella infection
  • Foot problems from wet bedding

Warning signs to call a vet

  • Lethargy or sudden death
  • Persistent diarrhea or bloody droppings
  • Abnormal eggs or shell defects
  • Respiratory distress or wheezing
  • Swollen feet or limping

Do not use human medications on turkeys—they can be toxic. Always consult a licensed veterinarian.

6. Daily care routine

  • Provide clean water and fresh feed daily
  • Clean bedding and remove wet spots regularly
  • Check turkeys for signs of illness
  • Maintain dry, safe and shaded outdoor areas
  • Collect eggs frequently

7. Summary

Turkey care requires attention to feeding, housing, water hygiene, and health. Proper observation, balanced nutrition, and clean housing ensure healthy birds, productive egg-laying, and disease-free flocks.
