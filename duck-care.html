Duck Care: Feeding, Housing, Behaviour, Egg Production & Common Diseases

For beginners, small farmers & backyard poultry enthusiasts • Reading time: 10–12 minutes
Poultry Backyard care Simple English

Ducks are friendly, hardy and highly productive birds. With proper care, they provide eggs, meat and can even help control pests. However, ducks require clean water, safe housing and a balanced diet for good health.

This guide explains all aspects of duck care: feeding, housing, behaviour, egg production, diseases, and prevention in simple, beginner-friendly language.

1. Understanding duck behaviour

Ducks are social animals that prefer to live in groups. Observing their behaviour helps prevent stress and early detection of illness.

Signs of healthy, happy ducks

  • Active swimming and walking
  • Bright eyes and clean feathers
  • Regular foraging and eating
  • Communicating through soft quacks
  • Normal egg laying in hens

Signs of stress or disease

  • Lethargy or isolation from the group
  • Pale or swollen bills and feet
  • Loss of appetite
  • Ruffled feathers or constant preening
  • Abnormal droppings or coughing

2. Housing and pond requirements

Ducks need a secure, dry, and ventilated pen with access to water for swimming. Proper housing prevents predators, disease, and foot infections.

Key housing points

  • Provide enough space: 2–3 sq. ft per duck inside the pen and 8–10 sq. ft outside
  • Secure fencing to prevent predators
  • Dry bedding (straw or wood shavings) changed frequently
  • Provide nesting boxes (1 per 3–4 hens)
  • Clean and shallow water for drinking and swimming

Temperature and lighting

  • Ducks are hardy but need shade in hot weather
  • In cold climates, insulate shelters and provide dry bedding
  • Natural daylight encourages egg laying

3. Feeding ducks for health and productivity

Ducks require a balanced diet for growth, egg production, and strong immunity.

Essential feed

  • Layer feed for egg-laying hens
  • Grower or starter feed for ducklings
  • Grains: maize, wheat, barley (in moderation)
  • Vegetables, greens, and pond plants
  • Fresh clean water at all times

Supplements

  • Calcium for strong eggshells (crushed shells or oyster shells)
  • Grit for digestion
  • Vitamins during stress periods or moulting

Feeding tips

  • Feed small portions multiple times a day
  • Remove spoiled feed promptly
  • Keep feeders clean to prevent disease

4. Egg collection and care

  • Collect eggs at least twice a day
  • Store eggs in a cool, dry place
  • Clean nesting boxes regularly
  • Monitor hens for broodiness and separate if needed

5. Common duck diseases and prevention

Early observation is crucial to prevent serious outbreaks.

Common diseases

  • Respiratory infections (coughing, nasal discharge)
  • Parasites: lice, mites, worms
  • Duck viral enteritis
  • Salmonella infection
  • Foot problems from wet bedding

Warning signs to call a vet

  • Lethargy or sudden death
  • Persistent diarrhea
  • Abnormal eggs or shell defects
  • Respiratory distress or wheezing
  • Swollen feet or limping

Never use human medicines on ducks—they can be toxic. Always consult a veterinarian.

6. Daily routine and hygiene

  • Provide clean water and fresh feed daily
  • Remove wet bedding and clean pens regularly
  • Check ducks for signs of illness
  • Maintain dry and shaded swimming areas
  • Collect eggs frequently

7. Summary

Duck care requires attention to feeding, housing, water hygiene, and health monitoring. Regular observation, clean water, and balanced nutrition ensure healthy ducks, productive egg-laying, and disease-free flocks.
